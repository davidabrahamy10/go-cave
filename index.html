About Caving

Caving is a great way to explore the natural world, but it can be dangerous. Caving or spelunking is a recreational activity that involves exploring caves. It has been practiced for centuries and continues to grow in popularity. There are many different types of caves, and each one has its own unique features. Some caves have stalactites and stalagmites that form from mineral deposits of water dripping over time, while others have crystalline formations that are created when minerals precipitate out of solution on the cave floor over time. There are also some caves that you can only get into by rappelling down into them from above, while others you can enter by walking in through an opening on the surface.
